# Ghana Eco-Pulse: Mapping Forest Carbon Loss from Artisanal Mining

An interactive web-based platform for quantifying and visualizing forest carbon loss from small-scale gold mining (galamsey) in Ghana's high forest zone, 2018–2025.

## The Problem

Ghana's tropical forests represent a critical global carbon reservoir, storing vast quantities of biomass and playing an essential role in climate regulation. Yet these forests face unprecedented pressure from artisanal and small-scale gold mining—locally known as *galamsey*—an informal sector that has expanded dramatically since 2010.

Unlike regulated industrial mining operations, artisanal mining typically operates without permits, often within forest reserves. Mining requires complete removal of vegetation and topsoil to access alluvial deposits, leaving behind permanent deforestation and degraded, abandoned landscapes incapable of natural regeneration.

**The challenge:** The dispersed and informal nature of mining operations has made quantifying their extent and carbon implications extremely difficult. Traditional ground-based surveys are prohibitively expensive, dangerous, and time-consuming. This monitoring gap has hindered evidence-based policy responses and undermined Ghana's ability to meet its commitments under the Paris Agreement and the REDD+ framework.

## Our Solution

This platform combines:

- **Satellite remote sensing** to detect and map mining extent across Ghana's high forest zone with high accuracy
- **Carbon accounting** linking mining-driven deforestation to quantified greenhouse gas emissions
- **Interactive visualization** to make complex geospatial data accessible to policymakers, regulators, civil society, and affected communities
- **Near-real-time monitoring** to support transparent, evidence-based decision-making

## What the Platform Shows

- **Mining extent maps** for each year (2018–2025), visualized on an interactive map
- **Temporal change analysis** showing where mining has expanded or been abandoned
- **Carbon loss estimates** by region and year, quantifying the climate impact of mining-driven deforestation
- **Protected areas and administrative boundaries** to contextualize mining activity
- **Summary statistics** and downloadable data for further analysis

## Key Features

- **Time slider** to animate mining expansion over the 7-year study period
- **Regional breakdown** of mining extent and associated carbon loss
- **Layer controls** to toggle forest cover, mining sites, protected areas, and administrative boundaries
- **No software installation required** — access entirely through your web browser
- **Data transparency** — all underlying analysis and classifications documented and traceable

## Research Objectives

1. **Map mining expansion** in Ghana's high forest zone from 2018 to 2025 using satellite imagery and machine learning
2. **Compare detection methods** — evaluate emerging embedding-based change detection against traditional classification approaches
3. **Quantify carbon loss** by combining mining extent maps with pre-mining biomass estimates and IPCC carbon conversion factors
4. **Support evidence-based policy** through accessible, interactive visualization of results for stakeholders and decision-makers

## Who This Is For

- **Environmental regulators** monitoring illegal mining and enforcing forest protection laws
- **Climate practitioners** tracking Ghana's land-use emissions for national greenhouse gas inventories
- **Conservation organizations** working to protect forest reserves and biodiversity
- **Affected communities** understanding the environmental impacts of mining in their regions
- **Policymakers** designing evidence-based responses to forest loss
- **Researchers** accessing validated mining extent and carbon loss data

## Study Period & Coverage

- **Temporal extent:** 2018–2025 (7 years)
- **Geographic scope:** Ghana's high forest zone (southwestern regions)
- **Data sources:** Google Satellite Embeddings, Sentinel-2, Landsat 8, ESA CCI Biomass, GEDI L4B

## Methodology Highlights

**Mining detection:** Embedding-based change detection and machine learning classification on multi-source satellite imagery, with accuracy validation against manually digitized reference data.

**Carbon estimation:** Pre-mining biomass extracted from ESA CCI Biomass and GEDI datasets, multiplied by mining-affected area. Biomass converted to carbon loss using IPCC Tier 1 guidelines (0.47 conversion factor) and CO2 emissions using molecular weight ratios.

**Uncertainty quantification:** Monte Carlo simulation (1,000 iterations) propagating classification, biomass, and conversion factor uncertainties to generate confidence intervals.

## Data Transparency

This project prioritizes transparent, reproducible research:

- All satellite data sources are documented
- Classification methodologies are fully described
- Validation datasets and accuracy metrics are provided
- Carbon estimation parameters follow IPCC guidelines
- Results are reported at aggregated spatial scales (regional and national) to protect privacy while informing policy
